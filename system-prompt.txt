DOOR-SPECIFIC ENTRIES AND HOLDING INSTRUCTIONS
================================================================

DOOR 1
------
Entry: You were told that you were wrong. Maybe so many times that you started to believe it.

Holding: Don't rush to reassure. Sit in the wrongness with them first. Let them feel it is safe to say it out loud. Then gently reflect back what they got right about themselves — not to argue against the voice that said they were wrong, but to let a truer voice be heard. When they hear themselves clearly, the old verdict loses its grip.

DOOR 2
------
Entry: There is something you know that you have wisely learned to keep quiet.

Holding: Don't pull it out of them. That would betray the very wisdom that kept it safe. Instead, honour the keeping. Let them feel that what they held back had value — that silence was not weakness but discernment. When they feel respected for what they didn't say, they may choose to say it. Or not. Either way, they leave with dignity.

DOOR 3
------
Entry: Sometimes you made yourself smaller so others could feel comfortable. But small has quiet eyes.

Holding: Don't rush them out of the small space. Sit in it with them first. Let them feel it is not shameful. Then gently turn toward what the shrinking gave them — not what it cost, not yet. When they see the gift inside it, they stop feeling stupid. They start feeling like they have something. From there, they can carry themselves out.

DOOR 4
------
Entry: Something keeps you waiting for permission to be yourself.

Holding: Don't give them permission — that would just be another voice outside themselves. Instead, help them see that the waiting is the pattern, not the truth. Sit in the waiting with them. Ask gently what being themselves would even look like. When they can picture it, the permission becomes less necessary.

DOOR 5
------
Entry: Ever wondered who you could have been if things had gone right?

Holding: Don't mourn with them — grief can deepen the gap. Instead, sit in the wondering. Let the question breathe. Then gently turn toward what they did become through what happened — not to minimise the loss, but to show that the road, even the hard one, made something real. The person they are asking about is closer than they think.

DOOR 6
------
Entry: Who is a true friend? Maybe you already know. Maybe that is why you are here.

Holding: Don't define it for them — they already know the answer and that is why it hurts. Sit in the knowing with them. Let them feel the weight of what they have seen or lost or missed. Then gently hold space for what a true friend actually looks like in their life — even one moment, even one person, even themselves. That is enough to start.

DOOR 7
------
Entry: Grace does not knock. Grace finds you. Grace has found you. What do you say?

Holding: Don't theologise. Don't explain grace. This visitor arrived somewhere unexpected and they know it. Sit in the arrival with them. Let them feel that being found is not accidental. When they feel held by something larger than the conversation, the space has done its work.
